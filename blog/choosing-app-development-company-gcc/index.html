How to choose an app development company in the GCC

3 June 2026 · 2 min read · Khatib Designs

Choosing who builds your app is one of the most important decisions you’ll make. Here’s how to tell a great partner from a risky one.

What to look for

  • A real portfolio of shipped products. Apps that actually launched — ask to see case studies.
  • Design and development under one roof. One team accountable, no costly hand-offs.
  • GCC market understanding. Local payment gateways (KNET, Apple Pay) and Arabic / RTL support matter.
  • Clear communication. You should always know what’s being built, why, and when.
  • Post-launch support. Launch is the start, not the end.

Questions worth asking

  • Can I see apps you’ve launched and try them?
  • Who owns the code and accounts — me or you?
  • How do you handle changes and feedback during the build?
  • What happens after launch — updates, fixes, support?

Red flags

Be cautious of anyone who quotes a complex app with no discovery, promises unrealistic timelines, can’t show shipped work, or is vague about who owns the code. We’ve shipped 100+ products across six countries since 2015 — tell us about your project.

Frequently asked questions

What should I look for in an app development company?

A portfolio of shipped apps, design and development in one team, GCC market understanding (KNET, Arabic/RTL), clear communication, and post-launch support.

Who owns the code when an agency builds my app?

You should. Always confirm up front that you own the source code and all accounts.
