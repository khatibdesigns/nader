How long does it take to build a mobile app?

20 May 2026 · 2 min read · Khatib Designs

Most well-scoped apps go from kickoff to launch in two to nine months. Where you land depends on scope and how decisions get made along the way. Here’s how the time is actually spent.

The phases of a build

  • Discovery & scoping (1–2 weeks). We align on goals, users, features and priorities, and turn them into a clear plan.
  • UX & UI design (2–5 weeks). Flows, wireframes and a polished interface you can review and feel before a line of code is written.
  • Development (6–16+ weeks). Building the apps and backend in iterative sprints, with working builds you can try as we go.
  • Testing & QA (ongoing + 1–2 weeks). Real-device testing, bug-fixing and polish.
  • Launch (3–7 days). App Store / Google Play submission and review.

What speeds it up — or slows it down

The fastest projects share a few things: a tight MVP scope, a single decision-maker on the client side who can give timely feedback, and ready content and brand assets. The slowest ones tend to grow in scope mid-build, wait on third-party approvals (payment gateways, integrations), or stall on feedback.

A realistic rule of thumb

A focused MVP is achievable in roughly 2–4 months. A full, multi-feature product is more like 4–9 months. If someone promises you a complex app in two weeks, be cautious — quality, security and a smooth launch take real time.

Frequently asked questions

How fast can you build an MVP?

A focused MVP is typically 2–4 months from kickoff to launch, depending on scope and how quickly decisions and feedback come through.

What slows down an app project the most?

Scope creep (adding features mid-build), slow feedback, and waiting on third-party approvals like payment gateways are the usual culprits.
