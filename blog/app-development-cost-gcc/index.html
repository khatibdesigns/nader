How much does it cost to build an app in the GCC?

12 May 2026 · 2 min read · Khatib Designs

It’s the first question almost every founder and business owner asks us: “How much will my app cost?” The honest answer is “it depends” — but that’s not very useful, so here’s what it actually depends on, with realistic ranges for the GCC market.

What actually drives app cost

Two apps that look similar from the outside can differ wildly in price. The biggest cost drivers are:

  • Scope — the number of features. Every screen, flow and integration adds design and engineering time. A booking flow, payments, chat and an admin dashboard are each a project in their own right.
  • Platforms. iOS only, Android only, both, plus web? More platforms means more to build, test and maintain.
  • Backend complexity. Real-time features, maps, payment gateways (KNET, Apple Pay), notifications and third-party integrations all add up.
  • Design depth. A polished, custom-designed product costs more than a template — and almost always converts better.
  • Compliance & localisation. Arabic / RTL support, data residency and industry rules (health, fintech) add real work.

Typical ranges by complexity

As a rough guide for a professionally designed and built product in the GCC:

  • A focused MVP (one core flow, one or two platforms) typically starts in the low five figures.
  • A full marketplace or on-demand app (multiple user types, payments, dashboard) usually lands in the mid five to low six figures.
  • A complex platform (fintech, real-time, heavy integrations, multi-region) can run well into six figures.

These are ranges, not quotes — the only way to get an accurate number is a short discovery conversation about what you’re building.

How to get more for your budget

You don’t have to build everything at once. The smartest founders start with an MVP — the smallest version that delivers real value — launch it, learn from real users, and reinvest in what works. It’s faster, cheaper and lower-risk than a “big bang” launch.

Working with one studio that handles design and development together also saves money: there’s no expensive hand-off, no “that’s not how we designed it”, and one team accountable for the result.

Frequently asked questions

How much does it cost to build an app in Kuwait?

It depends mainly on scope, platforms and backend complexity. A focused MVP typically starts in the low five figures, while a full multi-sided platform can run into six figures. The accurate way to price your project is a short discovery call.

Is it cheaper to build for one platform first?

Often, yes. Launching on a single platform (or as an MVP) lets you validate the idea with less upfront cost, then expand once you have traction and revenue.
