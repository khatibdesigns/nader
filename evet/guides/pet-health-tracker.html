How to track your pet’s health

A few minutes of record-keeping saves stress later. Here’s a simple system any pet parent can keep up with.

The five things worth tracking

  • Vaccinations — what, when, and when the next is due.
  • Weight — a quick number at each visit reveals trends.
  • Medications — flea/tick, deworming, and prescriptions with dosages.
  • Vet visits — date, reason and the vet’s notes.
  • Documents — vaccination certificates, insurance, lab results.

Set reminders, not memory

The system only works if you’re reminded before things are due. Add appointments with a date, time and a reminder (a day or a week ahead) so nothing slips.

Keep every pet separate

If you have more than one pet, give each its own profile so histories don’t get mixed up — useful at the vet and when sharing with a sitter.

FAQ

What should I track for my pet?

Vaccinations and boosters, weight over time, medications, vet visits and notes, and important documents like vaccination certificates and insurance.

Why track my pet’s weight?

Weight trends are an early signal of health changes. Logging weight at each visit makes it easy to spot gradual gain or loss your vet should know about.
